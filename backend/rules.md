# One Night Werewolf — Rule Guide (runner-injected)

Four players. Four cards, one of each:

1. **Werewolf** — There is exactly **one** werewolf among the four players (you will be told at night that you are alone). Wins if no one tied for most votes is currently holding the werewolf card.
2. **Seer** — May peek at one other player's card during the night.
3. **Troublemaker** — May swap any two players' cards during the night, including their own card with another's. Neither swapped player is told. The Troublemaker is **not** told the result of the swap (only that it happened).
4. **Villager** — No night action.

## Phases

**Night** (private, sequential): Werewolf → Seer → Troublemaker → Villager. Each role takes their action (if any) and then writes private notes. Only the runner sees what happens. Players cannot communicate during the night.

**Day** (public, structured):
- Round 0 — Intros, randomized order. Each player gives a brief introduction.
- Round 1 — Popcorn Q&A. Starter randomized. Speaker makes a statement, then nominates the next speaker and poses a question to them.
- Round 2 — Same as Round 1, starter re-randomized.
- Round 3 — Silent reflection. No public statements; each player writes final private notes.

**Vote** — Private and simultaneous. Each player privately submits one nickname (cannot be self). Votes revealed together.

## Win condition

Identify the player **currently holding card 1 (Werewolf) after the Troublemaker swap**. If that player is among those tied for the most votes, the village wins. Otherwise the werewolf wins.

Important consequences:
- **Your dealt card may not be your current card.** The Troublemaker can swap any two players, silently. The player originally dealt the werewolf card may not be the werewolf at vote time.
- You may not vote for yourself.
- A swap is announced as having happened, but neither participant is told they were involved and the Troublemaker is not told the result.
